Every organization has chaos. Most try to eliminate it. This project tried something different: making the chaos legible.

## The Problem

The client had 47 Slack channels, 12 project management tools, and a shared Google Drive that looked like a digital junk drawer. Nobody could find anything. Everyone was building their own shadow systems.

## The Approach

Instead of imposing structure from the top down, we mapped the organic patterns that had already emerged. Where were people actually going for information? What workarounds had they invented? Which unofficial processes were secretly load-bearing?

## What We Found

The chaos wasn't random. It had a shape. People had built surprisingly sophisticated informal systems — they just didn't know they'd done it.

We documented these patterns, gave them names, and built lightweight tools that amplified what was already working instead of replacing it.

## The Outcome

- Information findability improved by 60%
- Tool sprawl reduced from 12 platforms to 5
- Zero training sessions required — because we'd built on what people already knew

The lesson: sometimes the best design is the one that was already there, waiting to be seen.
