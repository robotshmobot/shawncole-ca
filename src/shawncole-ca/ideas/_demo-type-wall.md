The darkest setting. Near-black background with electric lime text creates a terminal aesthetic. Geist Mono titles in uppercase over Source Serif 4 body text. The contrast between the monospace headers and the serif prose creates visual rhythm.

## How it feels

Technical. Late-night. Like reading source code annotated with literary prose. The lime-on-black palette is high contrast and high energy without being warm.

> "Typography is what language looks like."

Mono-serif is the most unusual pairing. The monospace display font signals systems and code. The serif body font signals narrative and depth. Together they suggest writing about technology with literary ambition.
